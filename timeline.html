1958

In 1958, the Fairfax County School Board voted to reorganize the public school system and establish the county’s first intermediate schools. Traditionally, students in grades 1-7 attended elementary schools, and students in grades 8-12 attended high schools. Intermediate schools were created to ease the transition from elementary school to high school, and provide students with a specialized program of study geared to the specific needs of their age group.

1960

Mark Twain Middle School opened on September 6, 1960, and was one of the very first intermediate schools opened by Fairfax County Public Schools. Our school was officially named Mark Twain Intermediate School by the School Board in May 1959, and William E. Campbell was hired as our first principal.

1990s

In the 1990s, all intermediate schools in Fairfax County were renamed as middle schools.

2020

The COVID-19 Pandemic forced schools to adapt to remote learning. It was a unique time for the community, not only of Twain Middle School, but the world and humanity at large. During this challenging time which most of us were rather underprepared for, we still found ways to connect with and support each other as a community, signifying our resilience and commitment to education and the well-being of each other.
