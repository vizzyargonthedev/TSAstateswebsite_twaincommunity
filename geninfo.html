General Information

Mark Twain Middle School is a middle school in Fairfax County, VA, teaching roughly one thousand students in seventh and eighth grade. We offer classes that cover a range of topics, from required core classes to student-selected elective courses.

Core Classes

Core classes are required to be taken. Core classes include one of the Mathematics courses, as well as Science, English, Health and Physical Education, as well US History 7 (for seventh graders) and Civics 8 (for eighth graders). These classes cover the essential topics students need to learn in order to be successful in the future. Students are encouraged to take advantage of collaborative endeavors and group projects. Along with our International Baccalaureate Middle Years Programme, that focuses on many social skills needed in any given workplace, we have designed our classes to aid students of any academic level and skill to forge bonds between their fellow classmates, not only strengthening their academic skills, but honing the part of them that keeps our community thriving.

Elective Classes

Elective Classes have a different system than that of Core Classes. Students are given a detailed description of all available elective classes months before the school year begins. They are to choose six electives, ranking them by how much they prefer each option. If electives are not chosen, or if all six electives they have chosen reach their limit of students, the school will then choose the electives for the student. These classes are about topics that are more specific and career-based, allowing students to discover their passions before having to choose a realistic career path. These classes allow students to strengthen existing bonds and create new ones through more specific topics and experience as well as giving them the added comfort of being able to choose these classes themselves.
