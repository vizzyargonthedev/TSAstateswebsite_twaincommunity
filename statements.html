Mr. Taylor said that the hallway policies were “less enforced” in his opinion. He continued to say it would distract his fifth and sixth period classes due to those screaming in the hallway directly after lunch. Being a newer teacher, he did not have much else to share about his personal experiences at Twain. Mrs. Dehaven stated that there have been a number of changes in her time here at Twain. Most notably, the Yondr Pouch and Metal Detection Policies. Mrs. Dehaven herself has seen good coming from the phone policy, but respects the students’ and the parents opinions, saying it was “very split” on whether or not it was the right choice. On the metal detector policy, however, it seems “less effective”, not in detecting metallic objects, but in getting students to class on time when problems arise. For the most part, it gets its job done, and students are usually unaffected. Though regardless, it feels better to have some sort of precaution before anything problematic occurs in order to keep the community safe. Mr. Firebaugh noted that the Yondr Pouch phone policy seems to be more enforced, and the teachers and staff are “taking more responsibility” for it than since he first came to Twain in the second quarter of this past school year. He said that this was a good change, and he was content with the school's proactivity in trying to keep the phone use in school down, and that being more social and active with each other “would only benefit the community”.
